My Favorite Books





These are the books I consider true works of art. In particular for me there are 4 writers that stand above the others: Nietzsche, Leopardi, Cioran, and Gide. For me there is little distinction between the greatest and my favorites. This is because I believe in the power of judgment, that each individual must not only discriminate for themself but also find the inner voice that argues rationally and psychologically why this is the case. In our fragmented cultural landscape, only the individual aesthetic sense can rise up and create meaning of the world and life through sheer will and contact with the sublime. Although it may sound like an old cliche, I can find no more serious or invigorating philosophical stance than this. Of course there is always comedic cynicism, a viable alternative to be sure and here tantalizingly represented by Cioran and Houellebecq, but for me the heroic-romantic stance is ultimately more beautiful. Between and amongst these two stances lurks the spectre of Epicureanism, the ambiguous and powerful ancient philosophy of pleasure. It's no wonder writers like Hesse were perennially torn between the mind and the flesh. These books are the best I've read:

Human, All Too Human (Nietzsche)
The Gay Science (Nietzsche)
Zibaldone (Giacomo Leopardi)
Collected Aphorisms (EM Cioran)
Fruits of the Earth (Andre Gide)
The Immoralist (Andre Gide)
If it Die... (Andre Gide)
The Lord of the Rings (JRR Tolkien)
The Night Land (William Hope Hodgson)
Star Maker (Olaf Stapledon)
Complete Novels (Michel Houellebecq)
