My Favorite Books





I'm always searching out new books. Here are the ones I personally consider to be artistic masterpieces, the really sublime ones I've encountered through the years. You could say they form the core of my values and perspective, a kind of personal bible:

Human, All Too Human (Nietzsche)
The Gay Science (Nietzsche)
Zibaldone (Giacomo Leopardi)
Aphorisms (EM Cioran)
Reflections & Maxims (Vouvenargues)
Maxims (La Rochefoucauld)
Products of the Perfected Civilization (Chamfort)
Waste Books (Lichtenberg)
Characters (La Bruyere)
Memoirs from Beyond the Grave (Chateaubriand)
My Life (Benvenuto Cellini)
If it Die... (Andre Gide)
The Immoralist (Andre Gide)
The Possibility of an Island (Michel Houellebecq)
The Lord of the Rings (JRR Tolkien)
The Night Land (William Hope Hodgson)
The House on the Borderland (William Hope Hodgson)
Star Maker (Olaf Stapledon)
Narcissus and Goldmund (Hesse)
How are the Mighty Fallen (Thomas Burnett Swann)
Ishi in Two Worlds (Theodora Kroeber)
Dancer from the Dance (Andrew Holleran)
Candide, Letters (Voltaire)


And here are a few more that strongly influenced my life at one point or another, but for whatever reason I no longer feel quite as close to. These feel a bit more like a part of my past. They are certainly more idealistic, less cynical, less humorous you could say, and more overtly spiritual:

Voyage to Arcturus (David Lindsay)
The Idiot (Dostoevsky)
The Bhagavad Gita
The Dhammapada
The Old Man and the Sea (Hemingway)
Heart of Darkness (Conrad)
A History of Christian Thought (Paul Tillich)
The Perennial Philosophy (Aldous Huxley)
History of Sexuality, vol 1 (Michel Foucault)
Siddhartha (Hesse)
The Golden String (Bede Griffiths)
Sadhana: The Realization of Life (Rabindranath Tagore)
Walden (Thoreau)
Systematic Theology (Paul Tillich)
The Magic Mountain (Thomas Mann)
The Gospel of Ramakrishna (Swami Nikhilananda)
Autobiography of a Yogi (Paramahansa Yogananda)


And a few additional contemporary writers of non-fiction I admire:

Enlightenment Now (Steven Pinker)
You are not a Gadget (Jaron Lanier)
The Blind Watchmaker (Richard Dawkins)
Liberalism and its Discontents (Francis Fukuyama)
Dawn to Decadence (Jacques Barzun)
